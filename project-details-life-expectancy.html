Project Overview

Key Business Insight: Socio-economic stability and healthcare access are the strongest predictors of life expectancy, outweighing short-term economic growth indicators.

This project analyzes global life expectancy data across multiple countries to identify the key factors impacting population health outcomes. The dataset includes health indicators, economic variables, and social metrics collected over several years.

The primary objective was to understand how factors such as healthcare expenditure, education, GDP, and mortality rates influence life expectancy trends across regions.

"The analysis highlights that sustained investment in healthcare and education has a long-term impact on population longevity."

The project combines exploratory data analysis and machine learning techniques to uncover patterns, correlations, and predictive insights that can support public health policy and strategic planning.
